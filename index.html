Codeday Mentor Guide

Your Goals

  • Your primary objective is to help mentor students through the trials and tribulations of a project.
  • Help students solve logistical and technical problems so that they can stay working on their project.
  • Allow young developers to experience the local development community and what their future jobs will be like.
  • Ensure a safe environment for students to learn and thrive in.
  • Rotate around groups and see if they are having any problems that your unique skill set will solve.




Mentoring

  • Do... Ask open ended questions allowing the student to arrive at the answer.
    Student : - What is the best way to present our project?
    Mentor : - Tell me a time when you watched a presentation that you really loved.
    Student : - I loved when my teacher showed us how to do an experiment in science.
    Mentor : - What did the experiment look like?
    Student : - She showed us how a beaker could overflow if you put to much stuff in it.
    Mentor : - So seeing the beaker explode was your favorite part?
    Student : - Yea! I loved it so much stuff gushing out, I got an A on the assignment.
    Mentor : - So how could you do something similar with your project?
    Student : - I should show something that is really cool and that makes people remember the presentation.
  • Do more listening then talking. In general you should talk 20% of the time and listen 80% of the time. Think of Yoda :)
  • Do be ok saying, 'I do not know' to a question that a student asks you. You are not expected to know everything and there are a ton of people here to help each other with the correct answer.
  • Do show patience with the students. When listening give them your full attention, do not check your email and do not assume you know what they are asking.
  • Do encourage students to use the trello system that we setup for them.
  • Do give students encouragement about how their projects are going.
  • Do give students positive encouragement and prizes for how they are doing.
  • Do tell students what can be achieved in twenty-four hours to help manage their expectations.
  • Don't treat students as children, treat them as your peers.
  • Don't simply code the application for the students, let them type it themselves.
  • Don't bash or belittle projects that you do not like.
  • Don't dismiss or belittle the devices or technology that the students use.




Event Rules

  • Students are not allowed to leave the location without their parents checking them out. If you see a student leaving please inform Kristin Romaine as soon as possible.
  • Students can move items around the location as needed, and sit anywhere that they find space. Out of bounds areas are marked by caution tape.
  • Make sure that all aisle spaces are clear and that all wires are out of the main walking areas.
  • If you see someone that doesn't look like they belong at the event please inform the security members at UAT and Kristin Romaine as soon as possible.
  • Students will be provided with Labtops with Ubuntu on them if they do not provide their own.
    • Students may install software on these laptops. We will wipe them clean at the end of the event.
    • Students may not take these laptops home with them.
  • UAT will have security on site for the entirety of the event, they will be wearing blue shirts with the word SECURITY on it.
  • We encourage you to tweet but do not tweet or post photos of the students without express consent. ***???
  • If a student is disrupting the process for another group please find Kristin who will sort out any interpersonal conflicts inside of the groups.
